Climate Risk, Agricultural Productivity, and Long-Run Adaptation.

I study how climate variability and extreme weather shape agricultural outcomes in India, combining high-resolution climate data with applied econometric methods.

Explore Research

About

My research lies at the intersection of climate science and agricultural economics. I construct district-level exposure measures from ERA5 and IMD datasets (1951–2020) to estimate nonlinear temperature and rainfall impacts on crop yields and adaptation dynamics.

Research Areas

Nonlinear temperature damage functions
Extreme rainfall & dry spell metrics
Irrigation as adaptation
Panel econometrics & causal inference

Current Work

Climate shocks and maize yield responses
Adaptation mechanisms in irrigated districts
District-level climate exposure systems
