Climate Risk, Farmer Behavior,
and Economic Resilience.

PhD Research Scholar in Economics at IIT Guwahati working at the intersection of climate variability, agricultural risk, and institutional adaptation. My research integrates primary field evidence with high-resolution climate data to understand how farmers respond to increasing environmental uncertainty and how policy instruments shape welfare outcomes.

Research Agenda

Climate Exposure & Agricultural Productivity

I construct district-level climate exposure measures using ERA5 and IMD datasets (1951–2020) to estimate nonlinear temperature and precipitation effects on crop yields. Distribution-sensitive approaches such as MMQR and dynamic panel models reveal heterogeneous impacts across regions and production systems.

Risk, Insurance, and Institutional Design

My work examines crop insurance adoption under climate stress, evaluating participation decisions and welfare implications using instrumental variable methods and treatment effect frameworks. Particular emphasis is placed on endogenous selection and switching behavior.

Field-Based Welfare Analysis

Through primary surveys of over 400 farmers, I investigate how climate risk perception, institutional access, and production choices interact. This micro-level evidence informs empirical analysis of income stability, risk management, and adaptation pathways.
