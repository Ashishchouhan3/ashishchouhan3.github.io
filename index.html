About

I study how climate variability and extremes affect agricultural outcomes across Indian districts. My work integrates ERA5 and IMD climate data with panel econometric models to quantify nonlinear temperature exposure, rainfall shocks, and adaptation mechanisms.

“Climate change is already reshaping agricultural productivity — adaptation determines how costly that reshaping will be.” — David Lobell

Recent Work

Climate Shocks and Maize Yields in India
This paper estimates nonlinear temperature and rainfall impacts on district-level maize yields using ERA5 and IMD data from 1951–2020. I construct temperature exposure bins and extreme precipitation indices to quantify productivity losses and examine irrigation-mediated adaptation.
Extreme Rainfall, Dry Spells, and Paddy Productivity
Using panel fixed effects models, this study links consecutive dry days and heavy rainfall events to paddy yields. Results highlight asymmetric impacts across growth stages and the buffering role of irrigation infrastructure.
Adaptation Through Irrigation
This project evaluates whether irrigation moderates climate damages by interacting temperature exposure with district irrigation intensity, providing evidence on technological adaptation under rising heat stress.
