Ashish Chouhan

PhD Research Scholar in Economics · Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati
I study how climate shocks propagate through agricultural production systems and affect household welfare in developing economies. My work examines the transmission of climatic volatility into crop yields and evaluates whether formal insurance mechanisms mitigate resulting income and consumption risk.
Using district-level agricultural panels combined with high-resolution climate data, I estimate nonlinear yield responses across major crops. Building on this production-risk framework, I analyze selection into crop insurance and assess whether participation improves economic outcomes under climate stress.
