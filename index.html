Ashish Chouhan

PhD Research Scholar in Economics · Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati
I study how climate shocks propagate through agricultural production systems and affect household welfare in developing economies. My work examines the transmission of climatic volatility into crop yields and evaluates whether formal insurance mechanisms mitigate resulting income and consumption risk.
Using district-level agricultural panels combined with high-resolution climate data, I estimate nonlinear yield responses across major crops. Building on this production-risk framework, I analyze selection into crop insurance and assess whether participation improves economic outcomes under climate stress.

Working Papers

Climate Shocks and Agricultural Productivity
Full Paper
This paper estimates nonlinear yield responses to temperature and rainfall exposure across major crops in Madhya Pradesh using district-level agricultural panel data. The analysis documents heterogeneous production responses to climatic variability and establishes the magnitude of climate-induced production risk.
Insurance Participation under Production Risk
Full Paper
This paper examines the determinants of crop insurance uptake and extent of coverage. It analyzes how farm characteristics and exposure to production risk shape participation in formal insurance mechanisms.
Insurance and Welfare under Climate Stress
Full Paper
This paper evaluates whether insurance participation improves farm income and consumption outcomes. The analysis assesses whether formal risk-transfer mechanisms mitigate welfare losses associated with climatic shocks.
