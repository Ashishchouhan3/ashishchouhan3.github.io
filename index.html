Ashish Chouhan

PhD Research Scholar in Economics · IIT Guwahati Climate Economics · Agricultural Risk · Development

My research examines how climate variability reshapes agricultural risk and how institutional mechanisms—particularly crop insurance—mediate welfare outcomes under environmental uncertainty.

I combine primary field evidence from over 400 surveyed farmers with district-level climate exposure measures constructed from ERA5 and IMD datasets. My empirical work applies instrumental variables, endogenous switching regression, dynamic panel models, and distribution-sensitive approaches such as MMQR to identify heterogeneous climate impacts.

NET-JRF (UGC, 2018) · INFER 2025 (Italy) · Southeast Asia ERD Conference 2025 (Cambodia)

Research in Progress

Crop Insurance Adoption and Farmer Welfare under Climate Risk
Evaluation of insurance participation using primary survey data and endogenous switching regression frameworks to estimate welfare impacts under climate stress.
Nonlinear Climate–Yield Relationships in Indian Agriculture
Estimation of temperature and precipitation exposure functions (1951–2020) using MMQR and dynamic panel methods to capture asymmetric yield responses.
Climate Risk, Institutional Design, and Adaptation
Investigation of how farmer-level decision-making interacts with climate variability and institutional access.

Academic Engagement

My work has been presented at international and national academic forums including the International Network for Economic Research (INFER), the Southeast Asia Economic Research and Development Conference, the International Conference on Agrarian Change, and the Delhi School of Economics Winter School.
