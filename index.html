Climate Risk · Agricultural Production · Insurance and Welfare

I study how climate shocks propagate through agricultural production systems and affect household welfare in developing economies. My work examines the transmission of climatic volatility into crop yields and evaluates whether formal insurance mechanisms mitigate resulting income and consumption risk.

Using district-level agricultural panels combined with high-resolution climate data, I estimate nonlinear yield responses across major crops. Building on this production-risk framework, I analyze selection into crop insurance and assess whether participation improves economic outcomes under climate stress.

Working Papers

Climate Shocks and Agricultural Productivity
Estimates the impact of temperature and rainfall exposure on yields of soybean, maize, and paddy, documenting heterogeneous production responses to climatic variability.
Insurance Participation under Production Risk
Examines determinants of crop insurance uptake and extent of coverage, characterizing selection into formal risk-transfer mechanisms under climatic uncertainty.
Insurance and Welfare under Climate Stress
Evaluates whether insurance participation improves farm income and consumption outcomes, accounting for endogenous selection into coverage.
