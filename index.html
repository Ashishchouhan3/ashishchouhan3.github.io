About

My research examines how farmers respond to increasing climate risk, and whether institutional mechanisms such as crop insurance mitigate welfare losses. I combine district-level climate exposure modeling with primary survey data to study adoption behavior, productivity impacts, and income stability.

I conducted primary field surveys covering approximately 400 farmers for my thesis work, forming the basis for empirical analysis of insurance adoption and climate adaptation outcomes. My applied work draws on impact evaluation frameworks alongside panel econometric methods.

Methods & Tools: Dynamic panel models, DKSE, MMQR, impact evaluation • Stata, R • Learning Python

Working Papers

Crop Insurance Adoption and Farmer Welfare under Climate Risk
Using primary survey data from approximately 400 farmers combined with district climate exposure measures, this paper evaluates whether crop insurance participation stabilizes income and reduces yield volatility. Dynamic panel models estimate heterogeneous welfare impacts under varying climate stress.
Nonlinear Climate Impacts on Crop Yields in India
This study estimates temperature and precipitation exposure functions using ERA5 and IMD datasets (1951–2020). MMQR and DKSE approaches reveal asymmetric yield responses under extreme heat and rainfall shocks.
Climate Risk, Institutional Adoption, and Adaptation
This paper examines interactions between climate variability and institutional responses, testing whether insurance uptake moderates climate-induced productivity losses.
